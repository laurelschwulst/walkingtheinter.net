Our most recent NYC walk was held on April 4, 2023, led by pedestrian Alex Wolfe.

This exploration lasted approximately 1 hour, unraveling the hidden layers of the city's hidden infrastructure.

START: Muji Times Square

Muji is a Japanese retail company known for its minimalist design and commitment to sustainability. It's worth nothing the most calming meeting place in this area is a store.

Coordinates: 40.75621, -73.9903

NYT Building / Movable Type

Inside the New York Times building, the "Movable Type" installation by Mark Hansen and Ben Rubin. This artwork is composed of 560 small screens displaying excerpts from the newspaper's archives and live feeds from its newsroom, a dynamic embodiment of the evolution of print media in the digital age. The National Debt Clock is a constantly updating billboard-sized display that reveals the United States' gross national debt and each American family's share.

Verizon Headquarters + Plaza

The Verizon Headquarters and Plaza is an example of a privately owned public space in New York City. The plaza provides free wifi and charging hubs for public use.

Bryant Park, next to the main branch of the New York Public Library. Apparently four million books are stored underneath Bryant Park. Free Wi-Fi for park visitors, as well as access to the most serene and well-maintained public restrooms in the city.
